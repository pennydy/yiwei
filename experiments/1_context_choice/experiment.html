A cross-linguistic study of speech production and perception

Marc Garellek, Ph.D., is conducting a research study to find out more about how sounds are produced and perceived. You have been asked to take part because you are a native speaker of a language of interest, and because of your participation in an online platform such as Prolific or Amazon Mechanical Turk platform. There will be approximately 1200 participants in this study.
If you agree to be in this study, you may do any of the following: (a) read words or sentences from a list; (b) name pictures, objects, and/or events; (c) listen to words or sounds and/or view pictures. You may be asked to respond to stimuli (i.e., press a button, click a mouse) when instructed. You may be asked to learn unfamiliar words or sounds for unfamiliar pictures, and then you will locate the correct picture from a set of pictures as instructed. Each session will last no more than 60 minutes, but typically takes much less time, as listed in the description of this task on the Prolific website.
You will be compensated monetarily, as listed in the description of this task on the online recruitment platform that you were directed from.
There may or may not be any direct benefit to you from these procedures. The investigator, however, may learn more about how language processing works, and thereby be able to contribute to medical, therapeutic, and technological issues related to language use. Your alternative to participation is not to participate.
By consenting, you acknowledge that this study has been satisfactorily explained to you. If you have questions, you may reach Marc Garellek, Ph.D., at 858-534-2412 or mgarellek@ucsd.edu. You may contact the UCSD Human Research Protections Program at (858) 657-5100 should you have a question regarding your rights as a research subject or to report research-related problems.
Participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw or refuse to participate at any time without penalty.
Research records will be kept confidential by using an alphanumeric code, instead of your name, to identify your data files. Your data will be kept on a password-protected computer.
You have received a copy of this consent document to keep.
